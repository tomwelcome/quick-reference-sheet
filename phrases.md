Filho da puta: Literally "son of a b*tch." Can be used as a heavy insult or playfully between close friends.Arrombado: A very vulgar term meaning "ashole" or "motherfcker". It translates literally to someone who has had their anus breached, used to call someone incompetent or a terrible person.Vagabundo: Usually translates to "lazy bum," "thug," or "good-for-nothing." It can be very insulting depending on who you say it to.Cuzão: Translates roughly to "big sshole" or "coward." It comes from the word cu (ans).
Porra: One of the most common swear words in Brazil. It literally means "cum," but it is used exactly like the English "shit!" or "damn!"Caralho: Literally means "dick". However, it is mostly used as an exclamation of frustration or amazement (e.g., "Caralho! I can't believe it!").Merda: The exact translation of "shit." Used in the same way to describe something bad or express anger.Puta que pariu: Literally translates to "where the whore gave birth." It is the Brazilian equivalent of "holy sht" or "what the fck".
Vai tomar no cu: Literally "go take it in the as." It is a harsh way of saying "go fck yourself" or "f*ck off".Vá se foder: Literally "go f*ck yourself".Cala a boca: "Shut the f*ck up." While technically meaning just "close your mouth," adding tone makes it very aggressive .
Babaca: "A*shole," "jerk," or "idiot". It is milder than arrombado.Otário: Means "sucker" or "idiot" . It is frequently used when someone has been easily fooled.Pela-saco: Literally translates to "something that pulls the hair off your balls" . It is used to describe a very annoying, arrogant, or boring person .Corno: Translates to cuckold. It is a very common and classic insult in Brazilian banter.
